Albert Einstein

About the Scientist

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a theoretical physicist widely acknowledged to be one of the greatest physicists of all time. He is best known for developing the theory of relativity. His mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2 has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation".

He received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics.
